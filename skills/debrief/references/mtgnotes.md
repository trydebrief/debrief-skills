# /debrief mtgnotes

Turn a meeting transcript into a structured debrief: summary, decisions, action items with owners and deadlines.

## Apps
- **Granola** — pull the meeting transcript, attendees, and metadata.
- **Notion** — save the debrief under the meeting's project page.

## Step 1: Get the transcript
Use Granola to pull the most recent meeting transcript, or the one the user specifies. Confirm the title, attendees, and date before proceeding.

## Step 2: Extract structure
Produce:
- **Summary** (3–5 sentences, not a play-by-play)
- **Decisions made** (bulleted, each one standalone)
- **Action items**: owner, task, deadline (infer deadline from context; mark "unspecified" if truly unclear — don't invent dates)
- **Open questions** that didn't resolve

## Step 3: Present and offer to save
Show the debrief. Offer to write it to Notion under the meeting's project page (search Notion to find the right parent). Do not save without confirmation.

## Guidelines
- Quote directly when a decision's wording matters.
- Flag commitments where the owner wasn't named explicitly.
- Never fabricate attendees or decisions not in the transcript.
