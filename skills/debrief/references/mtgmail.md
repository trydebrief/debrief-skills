# /debrief mtgmail

Draft a personalized follow-up email that references specific moments from the call, ready to send from the user's account.

## Apps
- **Granola** — pull the call transcript and identify the recipient.
- **Gmail** — find prior thread for tone-matching and save the draft.

## Step 1: Context
Use Granola to pull the transcript and identify the recipient (usually the external attendee). Search Gmail for prior threads with this person to match tone — and to thread-reply if appropriate.

## Step 2: Draft
Structure:
- One-line warm opener referencing something specific from the call
- Recap of commitments (what they said they'd do, what you said you'd do)
- Any attachments/links promised
- Clear next step with a proposed time

## Step 3: Save as draft
Create a Gmail draft. Do not send. Tell the user the draft is ready to review.

## Guidelines
- Match the user's existing writing voice from prior emails in the thread.
- Quote something specific — generic follow-ups feel like templates.
- Don't invent commitments that weren't made.
